# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

One owner-curator (Przemek) and the friends he sends the link to. They arrive on a phone or laptop, idle and curious, with no goal more specific than "show me something fun to play right now." Nobody installs anything; every title runs in the browser in one click. The hub is the front door to a personal collection of 18 hand-built games that range from a 3D real-time strategy engine to a glowing loop puzzle.

## Product Purpose

The Arcade Hub is a showcase, not a utility. Its job is to make a personal game collection feel curated and worth exploring, to seduce a visitor into clicking into a title, and to carry a consistent identity across 18 wildly different games. Success is a visitor who plays a game they did not arrive intending to play, and who remembers the place it came from.

## Brand Personality

Theatrical, curated, hand-set. The voice of a repertory cinema programme or a festival lineup: confident billing, a marquee that announces what is showing, terse catalog metadata. Warm and analog rather than cold and digital. It treats each game as a billed title with a poster, not a tile in a feed.

## Anti-references

- Generic neon-on-black "gamer" aesthetics: glowing gradients, synthwave grids, RGB everything.
- Glassmorphism: blurred translucent panels used as decoration.
- Gradient text (`background-clip: text`) and other AI-slop tells.
- Identical SaaS card grids: same-sized icon + heading + body, repeated 18 times.
- Steam / itch.io storefront chrome. This is a programme, not a store.

## Design Principles

- **Bill the title.** Every game is announced like a film: a poster, a name set large, a one-line logline, a genre. The collection reads as curated, never as a dump.
- **Paper, not screen.** The surface is warm printed stock; the dark animated posters hang on it like framed stills. Contrast comes from the art, not from glow.
- **One marquee, then the index.** A single rotating spotlight does the seducing; the rest is an honest, well-set programme index you can scan and filter.
- **Metadata is typographic voice.** Numbers, genres, and counts are set in mono like a catalog, not hidden in chips.
- **Restraint with one bold note.** Bone and ink carry the page; a single theatrical vermilion is rare and therefore loud.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA. Body and metadata text meet 4.5:1 on paper; the vermilion accent is darkened where it carries small text. Full keyboard operability with visible focus rings, semantic landmarks and heading order, alt text on every poster, and a complete `prefers-reduced-motion` path that removes marquee auto-advance, staggered entrances, and poster animation.
